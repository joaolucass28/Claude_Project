# Copy Framework

Guidelines for writing website copy across key sections.

---

## Hero

**Goal:** Make the right person immediately feel "this is for me."

**Formula:**
- Headline: [Outcome] for [Audience] — clear, specific, no cleverness
- Subheadline: How you deliver it and who it's best for
- CTA: Action verb + what happens next (e.g. "Book a free call", "See our work")

**Rules:**
- Lead with what the customer gets, not what you do
- Avoid generic phrases: "innovative", "passionate", "solutions"
- One idea per headline — no compound sentences
- Test: can a stranger understand it in 3 seconds?

---

## Benefits

**Goal:** Show why your offer matters to the customer's life or business.

**Formula per benefit:**
- Short label (2–4 words)
- One sentence expanding on the outcome
- Optional: supporting detail or stat

**Rules:**
- Write benefits as outcomes, not features ("You save 10 hours a week" vs "Automated workflow")
- 3 is the minimum, 6 is the maximum
- Order: most compelling benefit first

---

## CTA (Call to Action)

**Goal:** Reduce friction and make the next step obvious.

**Formula:**
- Primary CTA: specific action + implied benefit (e.g. "Start your project", "Get a free quote")
- Secondary CTA (optional): lower-commitment option (e.g. "See how it works")

**Rules:**
- Never use "Submit" or "Click here"
- Match CTA to the temperature of the page — cold traffic needs softer CTAs
- Repeat the primary CTA at least twice on the homepage (hero + final section)
- Button copy should complete the sentence "I want to…"

---

## FAQ

**Goal:** Remove the last objections before the customer decides.

**Structure:**
- 4–6 questions maximum
- Lead with the most common objection, not the easiest question
- Keep answers under 3 sentences
- End the last answer with a soft CTA or reassurance

**Common questions to address:**
- How does it work / what is the process?
- How long does it take?
- How much does it cost?
- What if I'm not happy with the result?
- Do I need to prepare anything?
- Who is this best suited for?

**Rules:**
- Write questions in the customer's voice, not yours
- Avoid corporate tone — FAQ copy should sound like a real conversation
- Don't use FAQ to sneak in more selling — use it to build trust

---

## General Copy Rules

- Write at a 7th-grade reading level
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs. One idea at a time.
- Active voice over passive ("We build" not "Websites are built by us")
- Use "you" more than "we"
- Specific beats vague: "3-day turnaround" beats "fast delivery"
- Read everything out loud — if it sounds stiff, rewrite it
